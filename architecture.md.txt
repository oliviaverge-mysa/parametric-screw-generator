# Screw Generator Architecture (CadQuery / OCCT)

## Goal
Generate parametric screws as CAD solids with:
- Head (4 types)
- Drive recess (3 types, separate cut)
- Shaft (core cylinder + pointed tip)
Later: thread geometry + engineering drawing output.

Outputs:
- STEP (B-Rep solid)
- STL (mesh)

---

## Global Coordinate Conventions
All geometry is centered on the Z axis.

### Head coordinates
- Head occupies **Z ∈ [0, h_head]**
- The plane **Z = 0** is the **shaft-attachment reference plane** for *most* heads
- The “tool face” is whichever face receives the drive recess (defined per head type below)

### Shaft coordinates (local)
- Shaft is generated with its **attachment face at Z = 0**
- Shaft length extends away from the attachment face to the pointed tip
- Default shaft local orientation: either
  - extends to **negative Z**: Z ∈ [-L, 0], tip at -L
  - or extends to **positive Z**: Z ∈ [0, L], tip at +L  
  (Pick one and be consistent; if needed, rotate during assembly.)

### Assembly expectation (final screw)
- Screw is one watertight solid:
  1) build head
  2) cut drive (head - driveCut)
  3) union shaft (headWithDrive ∪ shaft)

---

## Allowed Head Types (ONLY these)
Head is built first and independently.

### 1) Flat (countersunk)
- Geometry: conical frustum (inverted cone)
- Large flat face (drive side) and cone side must be defined explicitly
- Must support “seamless” shaft join:
  - Shaft attaches on the **cone side**
  - The shaft attachment Z should be chosen where cone radius equals shaft radius  
    (so the transition is continuous, shaft top circle is “inside” the cone)

### 2) Pan
- Cylinder + spherical cap  
  - cap radius: r = min(d*0.25, h*0.5)

### 3) Button
- Cylinder + more domed spherical cap  
  - cap radius: r = min(d*0.4, h*0.8)

### 4) Hex
- Hexagonal prism
- acrossFlats provided or defaulted

No other head styles.

---

## Allowed Drive Types (ONLY these)
Drive is implemented as a **separate cut solid**.
It must be reusable with any head.

### Drives:
- Hex (size 3)
- Phillips (size 4)
- Torx (size 6)

### Drive Cut Rules
- Drive cut is placed at the head’s **tool face plane**
- Drive cut direction goes into the head (toward interior)
- Recess bottom shape:
  - **conical depth within the drive footprint**
  - Build cut as: (drive footprint prism) ∩ (cone/frustum)  
    not (flat pocket + separate cone)

No other drive types/sizes.

---

## Shaft (no threads yet)
Shaft is the core under the future thread.

### Geometry
- Cylinder of diameter d_minor
- Pointed tip at end (cone)

Parameters:
- d_minor (shaft diameter)
- L (shaft length)
- tip_len (cone height)

No threads, no helix yet.

---

## Attachment Rules
### General heads (pan/button/hex)
- Shaft attaches at the head’s underside plane (typically Z=0)
- Shaft extends away from the head to the tip

### Flat head
- Shaft attaches at the **cone side**
- Attachment point should be computed so that:
  - cone_radius(z_attach) == shaft_radius  
  (smooth/continuous outer surface)
- Use small overlap for robust union

---

## Smoothing
After union, apply a small junction fillet/chamfer to head↔shaft seam:
- very small radius (e.g., 0.1–0.25 mm) to avoid OCCT failures
- must NOT affect drive recess edges or tip edges

Fallback: if fillet fails, use chamfer.

---

## Preview / Gallery Policy
Maintain preview scripts that export:
- Individual part examples (STEP + STL)
- A single combined gallery STEP for CAD review:
  - head × drive × shaft (representative sizes)
- A sectioned gallery STEP to view recess + junctions

---

## Non-goals (for now)
- No threads yet
- No standards-perfect driver dimensions yet (visual + scalable first)
- No confidential references in documentation
